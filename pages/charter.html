Governance

Charter

The founding document governing our association's structure and operations.

Article I: Name and Purpose

The name of this organization shall be the Professional Association of Cambodia (hereinafter referred to as "the Association").

The purpose of the Association shall be to advance the professional standards, education, and ethical practice of its members within the Kingdom of Cambodia, and to represent the collective interests of its members before government, regulatory bodies, and the public.

Article II: Membership

Section 1. Membership shall be open to all qualified professionals and organizations who share the Association's commitment to professional excellence and ethical practice.

Section 2. The Association shall offer the following membership categories: Individual, Corporate, and Patron.

Section 3. Membership dues and benefits shall be determined by the Board of Directors and reviewed annually.

Article III: Board of Directors

Section 1. The Association shall be governed by a Board of Directors consisting of no fewer than five (5) and no more than twelve (12) members.

Section 2. Board members shall serve two-year terms and may be re-elected for up to three consecutive terms.

Section 3. The Board shall elect from among its members a President, Vice President, Secretary General, and Treasurer.

Article IV: Meetings

Section 1. The Association shall hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) each calendar year.

Section 2. The Board of Directors shall meet no fewer than four (4) times per year.

Section 3. Special meetings may be called by the President or by petition of one-third of the membership.

Article V: Committees

Section 1. The Board may establish standing and ad hoc committees to address specific areas of concern.

Section 2. Committee chairs shall be appointed by the President with approval of the Board.

Article VI: Amendments

This Charter may be amended by a two-thirds vote of the membership present at an Annual General Meeting, provided that the proposed amendment has been circulated to all members at least thirty (30) days prior to the meeting.
