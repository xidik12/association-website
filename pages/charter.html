Preamble

Founding Charter

This Charter establishes the foundational principles, governance framework, and operational guidelines of the Professional Association of Cambodia. It serves as the supreme governing document of the Association, to which all by-laws, policies, and regulations must conform.

I

Article I

Name and Purpose

The name of this organization shall be the Professional Association of Cambodia (hereinafter referred to as "the Association").

The purpose of the Association shall be to advance the professional standards, education, and ethical practice of its members within the Kingdom of Cambodia, and to represent the collective interests of its members before government, regulatory bodies, and the public.

II

Article II

Membership

1. Membership shall be open to all qualified professionals and organizations who share the Association's commitment to professional excellence and ethical practice.

2. The Association shall offer the following membership categories: Individual, Corporate, and Patron.

3. Membership dues and benefits shall be determined by the Board of Directors and reviewed annually.

III

Article III

Board of Directors

1. The Association shall be governed by a Board of Directors consisting of no fewer than five (5) and no more than twelve (12) members.

2. Board members shall serve two-year terms and may be re-elected for up to three consecutive terms.

3. The Board shall elect from among its members a President, Vice President, Secretary General, and Treasurer.

IV

Article IV

Meetings

1. The Association shall hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) each calendar year.

2. The Board of Directors shall meet no fewer than four (4) times per year.

3. Special meetings may be called by the President or by petition of one-third of the membership.

V

Article V

Committees

1. The Board may establish standing and ad hoc committees to address specific areas of concern.

2. Committee chairs shall be appointed by the President with approval of the Board.

VI

Article VI

Amendments

This Charter may be amended by a two-thirds vote of the membership present at an Annual General Meeting, provided that the proposed amendment has been circulated to all members at least thirty (30) days prior to the meeting.
